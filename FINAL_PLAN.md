# Final Plan — Hormuz Under Pressure

## Final story

The website follows a single question: *what can real public data tell us about pressure around the Strait of Hormuz?*

The story does not claim that shipping traffic caused oil prices, or that one dataset proves a closure. It shows three connected layers of evidence:

1. **Location and importance:** the Strait of Hormuz is a narrow maritime chokepoint with high energy relevance.
2. **Market signal:** Brent crude and jet fuel prices move upward during the relevant period.
3. **Shipping signal:** IMF PortWatch records daily vessel movements and capacity through Hormuz.
4. **Vessel mix:** tankers are the vessel category that makes Hormuz an energy story, not just a generic ship-traffic story.
5. **System comparison:** Hormuz is compared with other chokepoints, so users understand it in a global network.
6. **Evidence summary:** the final section states what can be concluded and what should be read as methodology limitations.

## Chapters

- CH·01 — The narrow gate: real Leaflet map and context cards.
- CH·02 — Market signal: Brent and jet fuel time series from FRED/EIA.
- CH·03 — Daily traffic through Hormuz: PortWatch daily time series with period filters.
- CH·04 — What kind of ships are we seeing?: vessel mix, not a duplicate of CH·03.
- CH·05 — Hormuz among chokepoints: comparison with 28 PortWatch chokepoints.
- CH·06 — What the evidence says: concise conclusion and data note.
