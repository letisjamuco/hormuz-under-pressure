CH·02 · HORMUZ vs THE WORLD

Why does Hormuz matter more than any other chokepoint?

There are 28 maritime chokepoints tracked by IMF PortWatch. The two filters answer different questions: one measures tanker volume, the other measures dependence on tankers inside each chokepoint's own traffic mix. Click a chokepoint on the map or a bar in the ranking, they are linked.

Tankers per dayThe average number of tanker vessels crossing a chokepoint each day. This is an absolute traffic measure, so very busy routes can rank high simply because many tankers pass through them.
Tankers as % of trafficThe share of all vessels at that chokepoint that are tankers. This is a dependence measure: it shows how strongly a route is tied to oil traffic, even when the total ship count is lower.
Circle size follows the selected metric. Hormuz in orange. Source: IMF PortWatch.
